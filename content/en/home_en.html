Welcome!

Welcome to the Know Your Rights for Life Website! In the current year of 2026, knowing your civil rights/ liberties allows for self-protection with the right to remain silent or the right to remain silent; prevents injustice by knowing how to recognize unfair treatment and allows you to assert your protections; allows for informed decision making by knowing what you can and cannot do within legal jurisdiction. Knowing your rights creates a just society since more people are aware of what can and cannot be done, and ensures access to basic needs and opportunities. Despite being so important, several surveys such as the one done by the Annenberg Public Policy Center in 2025 show that many people are unaware of these basic liberties.

   

Statistics

When participants of this survey were asked of some of their first amendment rights, the results showed the following:


  • 79% say freedom of speech
  • 48% say freedom of religion
  • 36% say right of assembly
  • 12% say the right to petition
  • 21% incorrectly say the First Amendment protects the right to bear arms (2nd Amendment Right)

    This meant that out of the participants:

  • 40% can name a majority of rights
  • 9% of respondents can name all five First Amendment rights
  • 32% can name three or four rights.
  • 42% can name one or two rights.
  • 18% cannot name any of the rights found in the 1st Amendment.
 

Takeaway of statistics

Despite being established 200 years ago, these civil rights are still important today, and it is now important than ever to know some of your guaranteed civil rights. Knowing your civil rights can benefit you greatly thorughout your day to day life.


In the current year of 2026, it is more important now and ever to understand the civil rights that are guaranteed in the United States of America.


Throughout this website, you will be able to explore some of your civil rights and examples of how they can be used throughout you daily life!
