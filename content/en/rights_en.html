What are rights?

Rights as "legal, social, or ethical principles of freedom or entitlement".

 

Pights describe actions that are allowed to people, regardless of their situation. Rights give people certain abilities and freedoms to use in their day to day life.

(This project refers to civil rights)

"Civil rights, guarantees of equal social opportunities and equal protection under the law, regardless of race, religion, or other personal characteristics."

Civil rights are important because they guarantee equal treatment, protection, and opportunities for all individuals, regardless of race, gender, religion, or background. They are essential to a functional democracy, providing protection against discrimination in key areas like education, voting, and housing. These rights prevent tyranny and promote social equity.

Where can you find your civil rights?

Your civil rights are wriiten on several federal documents, such as:

  • The Bill of rights

    • The Bill of rights are the first 10 Amendments of the U.S Constitution. This is the main document that contains the first civil rights. The Bill of Rights was written by James Madison, a founding father of the United States. The Bill of Rights was officially ratified on December 15, 1791. The Bill of Rights aims to secure personal freedoms of the people against the federal government./

  • The U.S Constitution

    • While the Bill of rights is apart of the Constitution, the Constitution itself has other amendments and properties that guarantee civil rights.

    The U.S. Constitution is the supreme law of the United States, acting as the foundation of the federal government, outlining its structure, and protecting citizen rights.
