After having read what are some civil rights and how they can used in you daily life, why is it important to use them in the first place?

So why is it important to know your rights?

  • Self-protection
  • Prevents injustice
  • Informed decision making
  • Creates a just society
  • Ensures access to basic needs and opportunities

Self protection

Understanding your rights helps you navigate interactions with law enforcement, employers, landlords, and others with positions of power, which can prevent violations of your civil and constitutional protections. For example, knowing you have the right to remain silent or the right to an attorney can significantly impact the outcome of a legal encounter.


Prevents injustice

When you know your rights, you are able to understand what is right and what is wrong.Therefore, it is harder for corrupt, discriminatory,
unlawful treatment to go unchallenged. By knowing your civil rights, you keep government institutions accountable for their actions. By knowing your civil rights, a baseline for fair treatment is established. You can challenge unfair treatment and/or stop violations of rights when they occur. Examples of this are illegal searches or self-incrimation.


Informed decision making

By knowing your rights, you re able to make educated decisions.Knowing your rights let you respond properly when faced with certain circumstances, possibly avoiding unintended legal problems or financial loss.


Creates a just society

When individuals are aware of their rights and the rights of others, they're more fair towards eachother. Values such as tolerance, equality and respect (13th, 14th, 15th, and 19th Amendment )are encouraged, which creates stronger communites and reduces social tensions.


Ensures access to basic needs and opportunities

By knowing your civil rights, you ensure that you have the right to have access to basic rights, needs and opportunities.
Some examples of this are:access to education, fair work opportunities, health, and an adequate standard of living.
