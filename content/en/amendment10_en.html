What does the 10th Amendment state?

In the Bill of rights, the 10th amendment is described as the following:

"The powers not delegated to the United States by the Constitution, nor prohibited by it to the States, are reserved to the States respectively, or to the people."

How does the 10th amendment work as a civil right?

The 10th amendment establishes federalism (a system where power is constitutionally divided and shared between a central government and regional governments)

The 10th amendment isn't an explicit civil right. Instead, it guarantees that the state, and therefore the people have liberties and rights that aren't guaranteed to the federal government.
