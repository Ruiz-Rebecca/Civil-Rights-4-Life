What does the 4th Amendment state?

In the Bill of rights, the 4th amendment is described as the following:

"The right of the people to be secure in their persons, houses, papers, and effects, against unreasonable searches and seizures, shall not be violated, and no Warrants shall issue, but upon probable cause, supported by Oath or affirmation, and particularly describing the place to be searched, and the persons or things to be seized."

How does the 4th amendment work as a civil right?

The 4th amendment protects people’s right to privacy and freedom from unreasonable intrusions by the government.

While the government has the right to search a person's home or occupied area, it must be done with a search permit and a valid reason.

People are protected with this civil right as people can be secure with their belongings, information, and person themselves.

What does the 4th amendment guarantee?

  • Protection from unreasonable intrusion (entering homes without reason)
  • A warrant is required to be searched
  • A probable cause (enough information) is required to search your home.

What is the Exclusionary Rule?

The exclusionary rule is a legal rule, based on constitutional law, that prevents evidence collected or analyzed in violation of the defendant's constitutional rights from being used in a court of law.
