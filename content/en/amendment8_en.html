What does the 8th Amendment state?

In the Bill of rights, the 8th amendment is described as the following:

"Excessive bail shall not be required, nor excessive fines imposed, nor cruel and unusual punishments inflicted."

What does the 8th Amendment guarantee?

  • No excessive bail: This prevents setting bail at an unreasonably high amount.
  • No excessive fines:This prohibits setting disproportionate penalties in the form of money.
  • No cruel and unusual punishment:Examples of this are Bars torture and barbaric punishments, poor prison conditions, overcrowding, and excessive force.
