What does the 6th Amendment state?

In the Bill of rights, the 6th amendment is described as the following:

"In all criminal prosecutions, the accused shall enjoy the right to a speedy and public trial, by an impartial jury of the State and district wherein the crime shall have been committed, which district shall have been previously ascertained by law, and to be informed of the nature and cause of the accusation; to be confronted with the witnesses against him; to have compulsory process for obtaining witnesses in his favor, and to have the Assistance of Counsel for his defence."

What rights are protected under the 6th Amendment?

The 6 rights protected under the 6th Amendment are:

  1. Speedy and Public Trial
  2. Impartial Jury
  3. Informed of Accusation
  4. Confront Witnesses
  5. Compulsory Process
  6. Assistance of Counsel



Speedy and Public Trial

In general, the speedy trial guarantees that the accused must be brought to trial or released within a reasonable amount of time.

This means that the government can't leave you in jail if you didn't do a crime for a long time, or that the government doesn't give you a fair trial in a reasonable amount of time.

Proceedings of the trial must be transparent as well. (Transparent means that it is public, and that it isn't secretive.)


Impartial jury

An impartial jury is a group of unbiased individuals, that make their decision based on the evidence provided in trial.

How is a jury selected?

A jury is selected through a series of questions asked to people when they recieve a jury summons (paper indicating you need to go to court). These questions are asked to see if the person has any bias or relation to the case. An impartial jury is based off of these questions.


Informed of accusation

Defendants must be clearly told the nature and cause of the charges against them.

What is self-incrimation?

Self-incrimination is the intentional or unintentional act of providing information that will suggest your involvement in a crime, or expose you to criminal prosecution.


Confront witnesses

People have the right to face and cross-examine witnesses testifying against them. (This is known as the Confrontation Clause)



Compulsory Process

Compulsory process is the right to call witnesses in their favor and ask them to testify.


Assistance of Counsel

Assistance of counsel is the right to have a lawyer for their defense, with the government providing one if the accused is unable to pay.
