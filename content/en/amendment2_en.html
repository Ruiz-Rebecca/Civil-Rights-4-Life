What does the 2nd Amendment state?

 

In the Bill of rights, the 2nd amendment is described as the following:

 

"A well regulated Militia, being necessary to the security of a free State, the right of the people to keep and bear Arms, shall not be infringed."

 

How does the 2nd amendment work as a civil right?

 

The 2nd amendment protects the right of the people to keep and bear arms. It was written as a precaution if the government needs to raise a local militia. The Supreme Court interprets this as the protection of an individual's right to possess firearms for self-defense.

 

2nd Amendment guarantees what rights?

 

The 2nd amendment guarantees the following rights:

  • The Second Amendment protects the individual right to possess firearms for self-defense, not just for service in the militia.
  • The federal government cannot interfere with the right to bear arms.

The 2nd amendment protects the rights of law-abiding gun owners.

  • The 2nd amendment protects an individual's right to possess firearms for self-defense, not just for militia service.
  • The federal government cannot interfere with gun owning.

Regulations of the 2nd amendment

  • The right to bear arms is limited to individuals who are at least 18 years of age for long guns and 21 years of age for handguns.
  • Federal law prohibits individuals who have been convicted of a felony from possessing firearms.
  • Federal law prohibits individuals who are mentally incapable from possessing firearms.
  • The right to bear arms is subject to certain location restrictions.
